<p><p>Preclinical imaging systems are used in research and drug development to visualize and study animal models before testing on humans. These systems include MRI, PET, SPECT, Micro-CT, optical imaging, and magnetic particle imaging (MPI). MRI uses magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of internal structures. PET utilizes radioactive tracers to measure metabolic activity. SPECT creates 3D images using radioactive tracers. Micro-CT enables high-resolution imaging of small animals. Optical imaging uses light to visualize molecular and cellular events. MPI measures the magnetic properties of nanoparticles to create images.</p></p>
