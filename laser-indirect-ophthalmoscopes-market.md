<p><p>Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscopes are used in ophthalmic procedures for diagnostic and treatment purposes. There are different types of Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscopes including Photocoagulating, Photo Disrupting, and Photo Ablating. Photocoagulating Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscopes emit a laser that creates small burns on the retina to treat conditions like diabetic retinopathy. Photo Disrupting Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscopes use a high-energy laser pulse to break down tissue, while Photo Ablating Laser Indirect Ophthalmoscopes remove tissue layer by layer with precision. Each type serves a specific purpose in ophthalmic care.</p></p>
