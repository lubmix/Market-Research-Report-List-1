<p><p>Knee osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint disorder that commonly affects older adults. It occurs when the protective cartilage in the knee joint starts to wear away, resulting in pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility. Knee osteoarthritis treatment aims to relieve symptoms and improve function.</p><p>The primary goal of knee osteoarthritis treatment is to manage pain and reduce inflammation. Non-pharmacological interventions include weight management, exercise, physical therapy, and assistive devices like knee braces or orthotics. Pharmacological interventions involve the use of pain relievers and anti-inflammatory drugs. In severe cases, surgical interventions like knee replacement surgery may be considered.</p><p>In recent years, the knee osteoarthritis treatment market has witnessed significant growth. The rising prevalence of knee osteoarthritis due to an aging population and increasing risk factors such as obesity and sedentary lifestyles is one of the key drivers for market growth.
