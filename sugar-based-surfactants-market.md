<p><p>Sugar-based surfactants are classified into three main types based on their molecular structure: monomeric, dimeric, and polymeric. Monomeric sugar-based surfactants are single molecules containing a sugar moiety and a hydrophobic tail. Dimeric sugar-based surfactants consist of two sugar molecules linked together by a spacer group. Polymeric sugar-based surfactants are larger molecules containing multiple sugar units bonded to hydrophobic groups. Each type offers unique surfactant properties and can be used in various applications across industries such as cosmetics, personal care, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
