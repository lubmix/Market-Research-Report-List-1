<p><p>Speciality fertilizers market types include Ammonium Nitrate, UAN (30%), Phosphoric Acid, Monoammonium Phosphate (MAP), Monopotassium Phosphate (MKP), Potassium Sulfate, Potassium Chloride, Potassium Nitrate, Calcium Nitrate (CN), and NPKs. Ammonium Nitrate is commonly used for crops requiring high nitrogen levels, while UAN (30%) is a liquid nitrogen fertilizer. Phosphoric Acid is used for crops deficient in phosphorus, MAP provides both nitrogen and phosphorus, and MKP is used for potassium deficiencies. Potassium Sulfate and Potassium Chloride are potassium fertilizers, Potassium Nitrate provides both nitrogen and potassium, and Calcium Nitrate contains both calcium and nitrogen. NPKs are complete fertilizers containing nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium in various ratios.</p></p>
