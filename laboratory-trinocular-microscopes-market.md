<p><p>Laboratory trinocular microscopes are classified into two main types: inverted type and upright type. Inverted type microscopes have the light source and condenser above the stage, allowing for observing specimens from the bottom, making them ideal for studying living cells and tissues. On the other hand, upright type microscopes have a traditional design with the light source and condenser positioned below the stage, making them suitable for viewing larger, solid specimens. Both types have their own advantages and applications in laboratory settings.</p></p>
