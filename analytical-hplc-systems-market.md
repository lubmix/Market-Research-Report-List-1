<p><ul><li>Normal Phase HPLC</li><li>Reverse Phase HPLC</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Analytical HPLC systems are commonly categorized into two main types: Normal Phase HPLC and Reverse Phase HPLC. Normal Phase HPLC separates compounds based on their polarities, with the mobile phase being more polar than the stationary phase. On the other hand, Reverse Phase HPLC separates compounds based on their hydrophobicity, with the stationary phase being more polar than the mobile phase. Both types of HPLC systems play a crucial role in analytical chemistry for a variety of applications.</p></p>
