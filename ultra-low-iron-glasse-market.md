<p><p>Ultra-low-iron glass is a high-quality, clear glass designed to have less iron content compared to standard clear glass. Two common types of ultra-low-iron glass on the market are rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass is made by pouring molten glass onto a metal table and rolling it to achieve the desired thickness, while float glass is produced by floating molten glass on top of molten tin to create a perfectly flat surface. Both types offer exceptional clarity and are commonly used in applications where aesthetics are important.</p></p>
