<p><p>Medium Density Polyethylene (MDPE) resins are commonly produced through two main processes: the Autoclave Process and the Tubular Process. The Autoclave Process involves polymerization under high pressure and temperature within a reactor, resulting in MDPE resins with excellent mechanical properties. On the other hand, the Tubular Process involves the extrusion of polymer through a tubular reactor, producing MDPE resins with high tensile strength and chemical resistance. Both processes cater to different market demands for MDPE resins with varying characteristics and applications.</p></p>
