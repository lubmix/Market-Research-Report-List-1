<p><p>Combination inhalers can be classified into two main types: pressurized metered dosage inhalers (pMDIs) and dry-powder inhalers (DPIs). pMDIs deliver medication in a pressurized form using a propellant, while DPIs deliver medication in a dry powder form. Both types of inhalers are used for treating respiratory conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). pMDIs are commonly used and require coordination between inhalation and dose release, while DPIs are breath-activated, making them easier to use for some patients. These inhalers are important in providing effective medication delivery for respiratory conditions.</p></p>
