<p><ul><li>CVD</li><li>HPHT</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Jewelry Grade Lab Grown Diamonds are categorized into two main types: CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition) and HPHT (High Pressure High Temperature). CVD diamonds are created using a process where a diamond seed is placed in a chamber filled with carbon-rich gases and exposed to extreme heat, resulting in the growth of a diamond crystal. HPHT diamonds, on the other hand, are formed under high pressure and high temperature conditions mimicking the natural diamond formation process. Both types of lab-grown diamonds are chemically and physically identical to natural diamonds and are increasingly popular in the jewelry market for their ethical and sustainable attributes.</p></p>
