<p><p>Noninvasive temperature management systems include patient cooling systems and patient warming systems. Patient cooling systems are used to lower body temperature in patients suffering from conditions such as fever, heat stroke, and cardiac arrest. On the other hand, patient warming systems are utilized to increase body temperature in patients undergoing surgery or experiencing hypothermia. Both systems are designed to regulate body temperature noninvasively, providing a safe and effective method for managing patients' thermoregulation.</p></p>
