<p><p>Hemoglobinopathies are genetic disorders that affect the structure or production of hemoglobin, the protein responsible for carrying oxygen in red blood cells. The market for treating hemoglobinopathies includes several types of therapies. Blood transfusion involves replacing abnormal red blood cells with healthy ones. Iron chelation therapy helps remove excess iron that builds up from frequent blood transfusions. Bone marrow transplant aims to replace the defective bone marrow with healthy stem cells. Other treatments include gene therapy and fetal hemoglobin induction. These therapies aim to improve symptoms and quality of life for patients with hemoglobinopathies.</p></p>
