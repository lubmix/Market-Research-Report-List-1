<p><p>Potassium caseinate is a type of protein derived from milk, used in the food industry for its emulsifying and thickening properties. It is available in two main types: sprayed type and extruded type. The sprayed type is produced by spraying a solution of potassium caseinate into a drying chamber, resulting in a fine powder. The extruded type is formed by forcing the potassium caseinate through a small opening under high pressure, creating a dense and slightly textured product. Both types offer different applications in food manufacturing.</p></p>
