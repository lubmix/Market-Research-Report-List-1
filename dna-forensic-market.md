<p><p>DNA forensics, also known as forensic genetics, is the application of DNA profiling techniques for identifying individuals involved in criminal investigations or other legal procedures. It involves analyzing and comparing certain regions of an individual's DNA sample with DNA from crime scene evidence, such as blood, hair, or saliva, to establish a link between the suspect and the crime.</p><p>The global DNA forensic market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 8.00% during the forecast period. One of the major factors driving this growth is the increasing importance of DNA profiling in solving criminal cases and identifying perpetrators. The advancements in technology, such as next-generation sequencing, have significantly improved the efficiency and accuracy of DNA forensics, thereby increasing its adoption worldwide.</p><p>Moreover, the rise in crime rates, coupled with a growing demand for justice and public safety, has led to increased investments by governments and law enforcement agencies in DNA forensic technologies. This factor is expected to further boost the market growth in the coming years.</p><p>In addition, the emergence of Rapid DNA analysis, which allows on-site and real-time testing, is a significant trend in the DNA forensic market. This technology enables law enforcement agencies to expedite the identification process, leading to faster results and improved criminal investigations.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing adoption of DNA databases, both at the national and international levels, is driving market growth. These databases store DNA profiles of known criminals, missing persons, and unidentified bodies, aiding in solving cold cases and providing crucial leads in ongoing investigations.</p><p>Overall, the DNA forensic market is poised for substantial growth, driven by advancements in technology, increasing crime rates, and a growing emphasis on public safety. The adoption of rapid DNA analysis and the establishment of comprehensive DNA databases are expected to further drive market growth in the coming years.</p></p>
